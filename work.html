History of the Oak Meadow Plaza Project

Here is a timeline with links to supporting documentation

Oak Meadow is a beautiful hillside area on the west side of Morgan Hill, visible from Community Park and the surrounding area. The Oak Meadow Plaza project intends to build approximately 48 homes in this area.

Oak Meadow is currently county land, but the developer has been trying to annex it into the city despite ongoing opposition from the community.

In 2004, Morgan Hill residents passed Measure C, limiting the cities ability to extend into county land until there is less than 5 years of inventory within the city boundaries. The 2010 general plan states "At current construction rates, available residential land in the city and in the UGB [Urban Growth Boundary] will take more than 30 years to develop." [Link]

In February 2006, City Council, and then mayor Dennis Kennedy entered into a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) [Link]. This was done without notification of affected residents.

In March 2006, after entering into the MOU, the City Council rewrote the law specifically to accommodate the Oak Meadow Plaza project by changing the City's criteria for adjusting the boundary "so that the proposal would comply with its provisions." [See page 14: Link].

After leaving office, Dennis Kennedy took a position as a consultant for the developer. [Link]

The developer put the project on hold in 2008, when the economy turned down, but started plans again in 2011 with strong community opposition. In 2014 the General Plan Advisory Committee (GPAC) discussed the Oak Meadow project and voted 14-0 in opposition of development. [See page 83: Link]

The GPAC joined the opposition of the Morgan Hill Unified School District [See page 75: Link], LAFCO (Local Agency Formation Commission of Santa Clara County) [See page 249: Link], the Audubon Society of Santa Clara Valley. [Link]

At the following Planning Commission Meeting, Oct. 14 2014, the Commission voted to deny the requested general plan amendments necessary for annexation. [Link]

Just one month later, Nov. 18 2014, the developer was back in front the planning commision and the decision was reversed. [Link]

On Feb. 4th, 2015, the City Council approved the general plan amendment and Mitigated Negative Declaration (MND) for the project allowing it to move forward. [Link]

We ask you to make your voice heard to the Planning Commission, LAFCO (a county agency that must approve the annexation), and the City Council, as we move forward with an initiative so that no more land can be annexed into the city without a general vote. Please help ensure that the Council listens to the residents and the community, and not only to the developer.
